General Information

Folding@Home is a distributed computing project that allows people to contribute to the research of Cancer, HIV/Aids, Alzheimer’s, Parkinson’s, and other diseases by volunteering their computing power to run protein folding simulations.

Started in 2000 at Stanford University, today it includes nearly 200,000 computers world wide outputting over 40 teraflops of computing power – effectively stronger than any super computer in the world today.

You can learn more about the project on the official website or on Wikipedia.
